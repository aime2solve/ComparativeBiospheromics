Comparative Biospheromics Network

Welcome to the Comparative Biospheromics Network - a national-scale initiative to study ecosystem resilience and biosafety through distributed ecotron research.

Synthetic biology is advancing faster than our ability to test its effects at the ecosystem level. Human activities are pushing systems beyond known thresholds. And most of our models still rely on tiny plots, simplified variables, or isolated lab work.

We need scalable, controlled, ecosystem-scale experiments. That’s what this network is about.

Over the past year, I’ve built:

  • A mock data dashboard for 50+ sites across the U.S., each with 4 controlled domes:
  • Control, +CO₂, +Nitrogen, +Heat
  • A plan to link high schools and universities in collaborative research and STEM learning called 200 Earths: https://www.aime2solve.com/home/200-earths
  • A vision for open-source, multi-dome biosafety testing infrastructure that serves both science and education

I call this approach Comparative Biospheromics - the study of ecosystems under parallel experimental conditions at meaningful spatial and temporal scales using a combination of biospheric and omics data.

Learn more about ecotrons and share with your senators: https://aime2solve.github.io/EcotronScience/

Explore data dashboards: https://www.aime2solve.com/home/200-earths/comparative-biospheromics
